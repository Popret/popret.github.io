The Value of Arbitrary Goals

I believe there is extraordinary value to be had in picking arbitrary goals.

Through some pop-culture osmosis I absorbed some absurdist philosophy like Camus which basically states “There is no meaning to life, and to look for a logical meaning from outside of the universe is fruitless.”

I also really value Self Improvement which by the logic of absurdism itself is absurd.

I picked up a lot from Beau Miles and his use of goals as ways to tell stories and to discover himself

In the face of absurdism, we are allowed to pick arbitrary goals and treat them as valuable as any other.

Picking goals “arbitrarily” is nearly impossible as we are the result of our past selves navigating a past environment, and so is any such “choice.” And if one does attempt to (via quantum-based-randomness or other non-deterministic process) choose a truly arbitrary goal I would argue the meta-goal of picking and achieving a truly random goal is itself not truly arbitrary.
